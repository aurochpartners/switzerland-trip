Swiss Trains: The Basics

What You Need to Know

  • Punctuality: Swiss trains are famously on time. If the schedule says 14:07, it leaves at 14:07.
  • No reserved seats: Most trains are first-come, first-served. Just hop on.
  • Platform displays: Look for the yellow departure boards. Find your train, note the platform (Gleis).
  • Tickets: Buy at machines, at the counter, or via the SBB Mobile app (recommended).
  • Luggage: No porters‚Äîyou carry your own. Storage between cars or overhead.

Mountain Transport

Rigi

  • Cogwheel trains from Vitznau or Arth-Goldau
  • FREE with Swiss Pass
  • 50% off with hotel guest card (you'll get one at check-in)
  • Schedules at rigi.ch

Grindelwald First

  • Gondola from village center
  • 50% off with Swiss Pass (~CHF 36 instead of CHF 72)
  • First Cliff Walk: FREE with gondola ticket
  • First Flyer/Glider: CHF 31 each (no Swiss Pass discount)

Jungfraujoch (if you go)

  • Eiger Express gondola from Grindelwald (45 min to top)
  • 25% off with Swiss Pass (~CHF 150 instead of CHF 201)
  • Weather dependent‚Äîcheck webcams at jungfrau.ch before going
  • Only worth it on clear days!

Train Etiquette

  • Quiet zones: Marked with a phone icon. Keep voices down.
  • Seats: Give up your seat for elderly, pregnant, or disabled passengers.
  • Volume: Swiss people are quiet on trains. Don't be the loud Americans.
  • Feet: Not on seats, even if they're empty.
  • Food: Eating is fine, but avoid smelly foods. Clean up after yourself.

The Swiss train system is a joy. Enjoy it.
