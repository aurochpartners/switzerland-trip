## Document Structure

### 1. Before You Go

**Passport:**
- Must be valid 6 months beyond return date
- No visa required for US citizens (up to 90 days)

**Travel Insurance:**
- Recommended for trip cancellation and medical
- Check if credit card provides coverage
- Swiss healthcare is excellent but expensive for visitors

**Phone & Data:**
- Best option: eSIM (Airalo, Holafly, or similar)
- Activate before departure or at airport
- Alternative: Swiss SIM from Swisscom/Sunrise at airport
- WiFi widely available at hotels and cafes

**Apps to Download:**
- SBB Mobile (trains)
- Google Translate (offline Swiss German/French)
- Google Maps (download Switzerland offline)
- Weather app (Meteo Swiss is local)

**Packing for January Alps:**
- Layers (temperatures vary dramatically)
- Waterproof jacket
- Warm hat, gloves, scarf
- Waterproof boots with good grip
- Sunglasses (snow glare)
- Sunscreen (high altitude UV)
- Power adapter (Type J)

### 2. Money & Payments

**Currency:**
- Swiss Franc (CHF)
- Approximate rate: 1 CHF ≈ $1.12 USD (verify current)

**Credit Cards:**
- Widely accepted everywhere
- Chip+PIN is standard (know your PIN)
- Notify your bank before traveling
- Visa/Mastercard preferred; Amex less common

**Cash:**
- Rarely needed but good to have some
- ATMs ("Bancomat") widely available
- Withdraw at bank ATMs to avoid fees
- CHF 100-200 cash is plenty for whole trip

**Tipping:**
- NOT expected — very different from US
- Service is included in all prices
- Optional: Round up bill or add 5-10% for exceptional service
- Never 15-20% like in US

**Prices:**
- All prices include tax
- What you see is what you pay
- No mental math required

### 3. Shopping & Daily Needs

**Grocery Stores:**
- **Migros** — Largest chain, good quality
- **Coop** — Similar to Migros
- **Denner** — Budget option
- Hours: Usually 8am-8pm weekdays, shorter Saturday

**Convenience Stores:**
- **Migros Daily / Coop Pronto / Avec**
- Found at train stations
- Open late, often Sundays
- Higher prices but convenient

**Sunday Closures:**
- Most shops CLOSED on Sundays
- Exceptions: Train station shops, some tourist areas
- Plan ahead for snacks/essentials

**Tobacco & Nicotine:**
- Available at: Kiosks, Tabakladen (tobacco shops), some grocery stores
- Smoking banned indoors in public places
- Vaping: Same rules as smoking
- Prices higher than US

**Alcohol:**
- Available at all grocery stores
- Legal ages: 16 for beer/wine, 18 for spirits
- No open container laws in most public areas
- Don't overdo it — Swiss drink moderately

**Pharmacies (Apotheke):**
- Green cross sign
- Some items OTC in US require prescription here
- Pharmacists can advise on minor issues
- 24-hour pharmacies in cities (Lucerne, Zürich)

### 4. Water & Food

**Tap Water:**
- Excellent quality — drink it everywhere
- Better than bottled in many cases
- Free public fountains throughout cities
- Look for "Trinkwasser" sign (safe to drink)
- If sign says "Kein Trinkwasser" — don't drink

**Restaurant Water:**
- NOT automatically free like in US
- Must order mineral water (costs CHF 4-6)
- Ask for "Hahnenwasser" (tap water) — some places will provide
- Coffee shops usually require purchase

### 5. Cultural Norms & Etiquette

**Greetings:**
- "Grüezi" (GROO-et-see) — Hello (formal, German Switzerland)
- Make eye contact
- Handshakes are common

**Volume:**
- Swiss speak quietly in public
- Americans often perceived as loud
- Keep voices down on trains, in restaurants

**Punctuality:**
- Extremely valued
- Be on time for reservations, tours
- Trains leave exactly on schedule

**Quiet Hours:**
- 10pm - 7am: No loud noise
- Sunday 12pm - 1pm: "Sacred quiet time"
- No lawn mowing, loud activities on Sunday

**Personal Space:**
- More reserved than Americans
- Don't strike up conversations with strangers
- Polite but not chatty

**Recycling:**
- Taken very seriously
- Separate bins for paper, glass, plastic
- Ask if unsure

### 7. Restaurant Etiquette

**Getting a Table:**
- Some restaurants: Seat yourself
- Nicer places: Wait to be seated
- When in doubt, wait at entrance

**Getting the Check:**
- Waiters won't bring it automatically
- You must ask: "D'Rächnig, bitte" or make writing gesture
- They consider it rude to rush you out

**Paying:**
- Pay at the table
- Credit cards widely accepted
- Splitting bills is normal

**Water (again):**
- You must order drinks
- Free water is not standard
- Order "ein Glas Wasser" or mineral water

### 8. Practical Details

**Electrical:**
- Type J plugs (three round pins, recessed)
- 230V (vs US 120V)
- US plugs DO NOT fit — need adapter
- Buy adapter before trip or at airport

**Time Zone:**
- Central European Time (CET)
- 6 hours ahead of US East Coast
- Jet lag: You'll be tired evenings, awake early

**Public Restrooms:**
- Often cost CHF 1-2
- Clean and well-maintained
- Free in restaurants if you're a customer
- Train stations usually have paid toilets

### 9. Safety & Health

**General Safety:**
- Switzerland is extremely safe
- Petty theft exists in tourist areas — normal precautions
- Violent crime very rare

**Emergency Numbers:**
- **General Emergency: 112**
- Police: 117
- Ambulance: 144
- Fire: 118

**Health:**
- Pharmacies for minor issues
- Swiss healthcare excellent but expensive
- Keep travel insurance info handy
- EU Health Insurance Card not valid for US citizens

**Altitude:**
- Jungfraujoch: 3,454m (11,332 ft)
- Drink water, move slowly
- Descend if you feel unwell
- Symptoms: Headache, nausea, dizziness

### 10. Switzerland Quirks & Tips

**Things That May Surprise You:**
- Everything closes on Sunday
- Trains are actually on time
- Prices include tax (nice!)
- Water costs money at restaurants
- No one jaywalks
- People don't chat with strangers
- Chocolate and cheese everywhere (great!)

**Insider Tips:**
- Sit on right side of train Lucerne→Interlaken for lake views
- Free water fountains throughout cities
- Train station shops open Sunday/late
- "Grüezi" goes a long way
- Swiss are warm once you break the ice
- Don't be loud — you'll stand out
