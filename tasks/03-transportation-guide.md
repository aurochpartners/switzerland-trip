## Document Structure

### 1. Swiss Train Basics (for first-timers)

**How Swiss Trains Work:**
- Punctuality expectations (they're famously on time)
- No reserved seats on most trains (first come, first served)
- Platform displays and announcements
- Where to find your car number

**SBB Mobile App:**
- Download before trip
- Buy tickets in advance
- Real-time departures
- Platform information
- Works offline for timetables

**Luggage:**
- No porters — you carry your own
- Storage areas between cars or overhead
- Keep valuables with you

**Etiquette:**
- Quiet zones (marked with phone icon)
- Give up seats for elderly/pregnant
- Keep voices down
- No feet on seats

### 3. Mountain Transport

**Rigi:**
- Cogwheel from Vitznau or Arth-Goldau
- FREE with Swiss Pass
- 50% off with hotel guest card
- Check schedules at rigi.ch

**Grindelwald First:**
- Gondola from village
- 50% off with Swiss Pass
- First Flyer/Glider: NO pass discount

**Jungfraujoch (if going):**
- Eiger Express gondola from Grindelwald
- 25% off with Swiss Pass
- Check webcams before going: jungfrau.ch

### 4. Lake Lucerne Boats

- All scheduled routes FREE with Swiss Pass
- Winter schedule: fewer departures
- Check lakelucerne.ch for times
- ⚠️ Weggis stop affected by closure

### 5. Local Transport

**Lucerne:**
- Walking city — most things accessible on foot
- Buses available if needed (FREE with Swiss Pass)

**Rigi Kaltbad:**
- Car-free village
- Everything walkable

**Grindelwald:**
- Village is walkable
- Buses to Bussalp for sledding

**Zürich:**
- Hotel is at train station — walk to Old Town
- Trams if needed (FREE with Swiss Pass)

### 6. Ticket Strategy Summary

**If NO Swiss Pass:**
- Buy point-to-point at SBB machines or app
- Use hotel Rigi guest card (50% off)
- Budget ~CHF 200-250/pp for all transport

**If SWISS PASS:**
- Activate on first day of use (Jan 20)
- Show pass to conductors
- Just hop on — no individual tickets needed
